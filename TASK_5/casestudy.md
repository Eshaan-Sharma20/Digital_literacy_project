# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive financial information. With the widespread use of digital payment platforms in India, such fraud cases have increased significantly.

A typical UPI fraud occurs in a step-by-step manner. First, the attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer in an online marketplace. Next, the attacker creates urgency or trust by offering refunds, rewards, or assistance with a fake issue. Then, the victim is asked to click on a malicious link or approve a “collect request” sent to their UPI app. Many users mistakenly believe they are receiving money, but in reality, they are authorizing a payment. In some cases, the attacker also asks for OTPs or UPI PINs.

Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payment systems are the most common targets due to their lack of awareness.

The consequences of such fraud can be severe. Victims may lose significant amounts of money instantly, and recovery is often difficult. It can also cause stress, financial instability, and loss of trust in digital platforms.

To avoid such incidents, users must never share their UPI PIN or OTP and should carefully verify all payment requests before approving them.
