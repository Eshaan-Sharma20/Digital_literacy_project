# 📱 Social Media Responsibility Checklist for College Students

## 📋 Checklist of Do’s and Don’ts

| ✅ Do’s | ❌ Don’ts |
|--------|----------|
| Think before posting any content online. | Do not share sensitive personal information (phone, address, passwords). |
| Use strong privacy settings on your social media accounts. | Avoid posting offensive, abusive, or inappropriate content. |
| Verify information before sharing to prevent misinformation. | Do not engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or harassment. |
| Maintain a positive and professional digital footprint. | Avoid clicking or sharing suspicious or unknown links. |
| Communicate respectfully in comments and messages. | Do not overshare your location or daily activities publicly. |

---

## 📝 Note
Social media is a powerful tool for communication, networking, and self-expression. However, irresponsible use can lead to serious consequences such as privacy breaches, reputational damage, or cybercrime risks. College students must be aware that their online behavior reflects their personality and can impact future academic and career opportunities.

---

## ✅ Conclusion
Responsible use of social media is essential in today’s digital world. By following basic do’s and don’ts, students can protect their personal information, maintain a positive online presence, and avoid unnecessary risks. Practicing digital responsibility ensures safe, respectful, and productive use of social platforms.
